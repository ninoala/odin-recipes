Egg and Spinach Sandwich

Description

These delicious egg sandwiches packed with vegetables are a nutricious breakfast full of proteins that is easy to prepare in advance. Just freeze prepared sandwiches and warm them up as needed to make up for that precious morning time of yours.

You can also make this sandwich vegeterian. Just use a plant-based egg alternative and there you go!

Ingredients

  • Eggs
  • Milk
  • Spinach
  • English muffins
  • Parchment paper or foil
  • Olive oil or olive oil spray
  • Cheese (optional but obviously recommended)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Onion powder
  • Oil (I prefer extra virgin olive oil)

Steps

  1. Saute the spinach in oil (or water) in a large skillet over medium heat. Transfer the spinach to a bowl or colander to cool.
  2. Toast the English muffins.
  3. Whisk the eggs, milk, onion powder, salt and pepper in a medium mixing bowl until combined. Add the cooled spinach. (You might drain the spinach to avoid discoloring the egg mixture. But that's just aesthetics)
  4. Spray a sheet pan generously with olive oil. Pour the egg and spinach mixture into the sheet pan. Bake at 350 F about 20 minutes until the eggs are cooked through.
  5. Remove the eggs from the oven and cool completely. Cut into 6 equal squares. Split and toast the English muffins.
  6. Place the base of each English muffin on each of the pieces of parchment (or foil). Top each with one egg patty, a cheddar slice, and the top of the English muffin. Wrap the foil around the sandwich. Refrigerate and enjoy within 2 days or place all sandwiches into a freezer-safe bag or container to freeze. Enjoy within about 3 months.
